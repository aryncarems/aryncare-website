PF & ESI Compliance Guide for Contract Staffing in India

2026-06-22 7 min read Aryncare Editorial Team

Contract staffing gives businesses flexibility, but it doesn't remove statutory obligations, it just changes who's responsible for meeting them. Understanding exactly how PF and ESI apply to contract labour is essential, both to stay compliant and to evaluate whether a staffing partner is actually doing their job.

Who is responsible for PF and ESI on contract staff?

Under the Contract Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Act, the principal employer (your business) carries ultimate responsibility for ensuring contract workers receive statutory benefits, even when a staffing agency is the direct employer of record. In practice, this means the staffing agency handles day-to-day compliance, but your business remains liable if that compliance fails.

Provident Fund (PF) basics

Any establishment with 20 or more employees is generally required to register under the Employees' Provident Fund scheme. Both employer and employee contribute a percentage of basic wages, currently 12% each, toward the employee's PF account. For contract staff, the staffing agency, as the employer of record, is responsible for registration, monthly contributions and filing, but your business should request monthly PF challans as proof of compliance.

Employee State Insurance (ESI) basics

ESI applies to employees earning up to a specified wage ceiling and provides medical and cash benefits during sickness, maternity and employment injury. Establishments with 10 or more employees (in most states) fall under ESI's scope. As with PF, the staffing agency manages registration and contributions for contract staff, but documentation should flow back to the principal employer.

What to actually check with your staffing partner

Ask for monthly PF and ESI challans, not just an assurance that "compliance is handled." Request the Labour Licence number under the Contract Labour Act for any staffing vendor supplying more than the threshold number of workers. Confirm minimum wage compliance against the current Tamil Nadu minimum wage notification for the specific job category. Ask whether statutory registers, Form V, Form VI-A and others, are being maintained and available for inspection.

The cost of getting this wrong

Non-compliance isn't just a paperwork risk. Labour department inspections can result in penalties, and in the worst cases, personal liability for company directors under certain provisions. More commonly, it surfaces during due diligence for funding rounds, acquisitions or large client contracts, where a compliance gap can stall a deal.
